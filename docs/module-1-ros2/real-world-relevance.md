# Real-world relevance and learning outcomes

## Overview

Understanding the real-world applications of ROS 2 in humanoid robotics helps contextualize the technical concepts learned in this module. This section connects the theoretical knowledge to practical implementations in research and industry.

## Real-World Applications of ROS 2 in Humanoid Robotics

### Research Institutions
- **NASA**: Valkyrie and Robonaut humanoid robots use ROS for control
- **Honda**: ASIMO and 3E robot series utilize ROS-based architectures
- **Toyota**: HSR (Human Support Robot) and other assistive robots
- **University Labs**: Most humanoid robotics research uses ROS for development

### Industrial Applications
- **Service Robots**: Hotel concierge, restaurant service, and retail assistance
- **Healthcare**: Patient assistance and rehabilitation support
- **Manufacturing**: Collaborative robots working alongside humans
- **Disaster Response**: Search and rescue operations

### Competition Robotics
- **RoboCup**: Humanoid league standardizes on ROS
- **DARPA Robotics Challenge**: Many teams used ROS for control
- **Amazon Picking Challenge**: Manipulation-focused humanoid tasks

## Key Technologies and Companies

### Major Players
- **Boston Dynamics**: Spot and Atlas robots (uses ROS-compatible communication)
- **SoftBank Robotics**: Pepper and NAO robots
- **ABB**: YuMi collaborative robot system
- **KUKA**: LBR iiwa and other collaborative robots

### Open Source Projects
- **ROS-Industrial**: Industrial robotics applications
- **MoveIt**: Motion planning for manipulation
- **navigation2**: Path planning and navigation
- **Gazebo**: Physics simulation for testing
